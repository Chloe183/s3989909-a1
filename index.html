All houses where men once lived and died 
Are haunted still. Through silent doors 
The harmless phantoms softly glide, 
Their footsteps echo on the floors.

Let's explore deeper...

We sense them on the narrow stair, 
Or drifting through the empty hall— 
Impressions stirring in the air, 
Like whispers faint and musical. 
        
At every meal, though none may see, 
Are quiet guests we never named. 
Their presence lingers endlessly, 
As still as portraits, unashamed. 
        
The stranger sees but walls and light, 
While I behold what used to be— 
The past remains within my sight, 
Alive with ghostly memory.
